## First whole year at University of Waterloo

### 1A term

#### Course Load

I have taken following courses in my first term at UWaterloo:

|Course Code|Course Description|
|:---:|---|
|ARTS 190|First-Year Topics in Arts Disciplines|
|ECE 105|Classical Mechanics|
|ECE 150|Fundamentals of Programming|
|ECE 190|Engineering Profession and Practice|
|ECE 198|Project Studio|
|MATH 115|Linear Algebra for Engineering|
|MATH 117|Calculus 1 for Engineering|

I honestly didn't enjoy the term. Not that the courses were super hard, but there was a big bump in workload in comparison to secondary school.
My favorite courses this term were **ECE 150** and **ECE 198**:

- **ECE 150** was basically learning things I already knew fundamentally.
- **ECE 198** was learning how to make actual products and things that can work in the real world.


Quite Obviously, my least favorite courses were **MATH 117** and **ARTS 190**.
> And I will not explain why. 🤫

#### Residence and Food

II was placed in **WEN north**, right next to my don's room. 

Thankfully, I had a kitchen in my residence room, so I was able to cook my own meals. However, since it was mandatory to buy a meal plan, I used the cafeteria for breakfast, snacks, and late supper. 

* **My go-to menu:** Booster Juice and pasta (sometimes burgers and pizza when I was up late).
* **Room condition:** The room was not bad, and the heating was super nice. **However, there was no AC whatsoever**, so I had to shower multiple times a day.
* **The Shower Struggle:** The washrooms and showers were shared, so sometimes I had to run to my friend's room when I needed a quick shower.

#### Gears

In my 1A term, I used an **iPad Pro 11-inch (M1)** for my note-taking, alongside my old **MacBook Pro 13-inch** (2018, 4-port base model).

However, during my finals week, **my laptop decided to stop working.** 
I had to emergency-buy a new **MacBook Pro 16-inch (M1 Pro)**. It was the only available option at the moment, and I had no choice.

### CO-OP 1

Co-operative Work Term

#### WaterlooWorks

WaterlooWorks was a pure disaster for me. There was no practical guide that I could follow at the time, and only a partial guide was available.

Also, I didn't notice there were **application limits** on how many positions I could apply for.

- **The First Round:** I only applied to Big Tech companies and completely ran out of my application caps.
- **The Server Crash:** To make matters worse, the server became slower as the deadline approached. At some point, it just decided to fail on me.

It was a disaster on my first run, but I learned a lot from it. Thankfully, during the continuous cycle, I was able to get an interview and finally land a job.

#### Actual Work

For my first co-op, I worked at **Stackpole International** as a **Software Developer Co-op Student**.

I was really hoping for a remote position, as commuting from Waterloo to Hamilton is quite tough for someone without a car, especially during COVID. However, it was a strictly in-person role, and there was no room for negotiation.

**My Role & Tech Stack:**
My job involved working with an early-stage specialized AI, utilizing a **reinforcement decision tree** to detect defects during production on local edge devices like the **Jetson Nano**.

It was an incredibly interactive experience. I got to see what a real software engineering position feels like, along with the real-world possibilities of AI.
